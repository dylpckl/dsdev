# RapidPay

Here are my design notes on a complex form where the user goal is to validate information populated by OCR scanning software. I leaned heavily on progressive disclosure to make this monster of a form much more digestable. Let me know what you think!

Dylan 👋

Design Goals
Design a feature that translates the functionality of the legacy page into the web version of the software.
Improve user flow and make the form easier to interact with.
Keep the two column layout with image on left and form on right.
Components
Accordions that automatically collpase when a user is done with them is a great way to utilize progressive disclosure to keep the complex form easily digestable. Additionally, only the accordions that require attention are expanded when the page is fetched. Nice!
Badges in the accordion header communicate key pieces of information and display validation errors (even when the accordion is collapsed!)
Cards are a fun and effective way to display multiple pieces of information with a visual hierarchy on what is essentially a big button. A subtle footer is used for edit/delete actions.
